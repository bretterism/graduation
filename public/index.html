I'm Graduating!

It has been a long time since I began my college education, but the time has finally come. I am graduating! Thank you everyone who have given your love and support throughout all these years. I couldn’t have made it without you.

When I first started college, I really had no idea what I wanted out of it. I enrolled at Lane Community College with a major in graphic design. I picked this major because it was a way for me to enter web design. After about a year and a half, I had realized that graphic design was not for me. I was pretty bad at drawing, and wasn't really enjoying my classes.

Around this same time, I had been spending most of my evenings on projects in the garage. I had built potato guns, a table with an interactive screen for the top, and even gotten into fixing video game consoles and other electronics to make a profit. One day at work I was telling my co-worker how I felt about my schooling, to which he replied, “Why don't you go into engineering?”

It didn't occur to me before then that I could actually make a career out of the things I love doing. After a few weeks of consideration, I decided to switch career paths to pursue engineering.

I spent the next few years at LCC to improve my math skills. At that point they were below even the college level of algebra required by most majors. I gradually learned more and more until I got through basic calculus. At this point it was finally time to move to Corvallis and attend Oregon State University.

Moving to Corvallis was one of the biggest leaps in life. I was moving out of my parents house to live in a different town, with no roommates, where I knew nobody, in order to attend a university. I felt very intimidated for the first few months. I wasn't really sure if I could make it in this town. After about a month of not establishing any relationships, I finally broke out of my shell and got a hold of the one person I sort of knew. She was a sister of a friend of mine, and at that point I was wanting nothing more than to make a friend. I met Kathleen at a coffee shop on campus for lunch. It was awkward to say the least, but I was thankful just to have a friend. All of you know where that relationship has blossomed.

After four years in Corvallis, I have come out of my shell, made lots of friends, got married, and am about to graduate. There were many times when I felt like giving up. But between my friends, family, and God always encouraging me I have reached the end of my goal. I thank everyone again who has helped me get to this point, and I am very excited to have made it!

Where We Are Going

We are moving to the Portland area! A month after graduating, I will start my new career path at Huron Consulting Group. They work closely with hospitals and health care systems to provide software custom tailored to their needs. I will be working mostly with databases and also in customer relations. I start in mid July, when they will be sending me to Chicago for training. After a few weeks of training I will be back in Oregon and working full time in Lake Oswego.

Before we head up to Portland, Kathleen and I will be taking some time off so that we can grow closer together, and enjoy some vacation time. We are currently planning a backpacking trip in the Eagle Cap Wilderness in eastern Oregon. We are preparing for a 5 day, 40 mile trip full of excitement and adventures!
